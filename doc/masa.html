MASA

MASA is a 9 tone "drawbar" organ with the harmonic gamut of the Hammond B3.

The name MASA is from the book We Can Build You by Philip K Dick.
With their market for organs drying up, the Multiplexed Acoustical Systems of America switched to the manufacture of simulacrum. The prototype was an Abraham Lincoln product. Upon completion the Lincoln immediately resumed the practice of law....

The 3x9 block of sliders on the left control the individual tones. The top row sets the tone amplitude. The middle row sets how much the envelope generator effects the tone level. The bottom row sets how much the external control signal effects to tone level. The external control only has effect if the tone level is non zero.

The envelope has a simple Attack Release contour without a sustain portion.

The vibrato section offers no surprises:
Freq LFO frequency
Delay Vibrato onset delay
Sens Vibrato sensitivity
Depth Vibrato depth

The external section controls are:
Bias Fixed value added to external signal
Scale Scale factor
Pitch How much external control signal is applied to pitch
